1. Watch the intro video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Xpk67YzOn5w

2. Display slideshow to review base 10 (normal numbers), then introduce binary (1s and 0s).

3. Explain how hexadecimal is a shortcut for writing long binary numbers (base 16).

4. Practice converting between binary, decimal, and hex using problems built into the slides (no worksheet).

5. Show the ASCII table and explain how each letter is represented by a number.

6. Play a Kahoot quiz to practice decoding binary and hexadecimal ASCII codes.

7. Split students into teams and hand out the printed decoding challenge.

8. Teams race to decode all three messages: two binary and one hex. First correct team wins candy and joins the Hall of Fame.
